# Weekly Retro

A weekly retro turns experience into compounding wisdom — or it doesn't, if you skip it.

## 1. Inventory the week (factual)

Before reflecting, gather facts. Use these tools:

- `calendar_list_events` — what meetings happened?
- `github_list_commits` (if applicable) — what code shipped?
- `slack_channel_history` — what was discussed?
- `notion_search` (if used) — what was documented?

This anchors retro in reality, not memory.

## 2. The 4-quadrant retro

### ⭐ Wins
- What went well?
- What did I do that I should keep doing?
- What did someone else do that I should call out?

Specific > general:
- ❌ "Had a productive week"
- ✅ "Shipped trading dashboard to production, got positive feedback from 3 customers, learned new Anchor pattern"

### 🚧 Frustrations
- What went poorly?
- What blocked me?
- What dragged on too long?

Be honest, not self-flagellating.

### 💡 Learnings
- What surprised me?
- What did I learn about [me / others / the work / the market]?
- What would I do differently if I could rerun?

A learning is something you'll act on. If it's not actionable, it's an observation.

### 🎯 Next week priorities
- What's the ONE thing that, if done, makes next week a success?
- What 2-3 secondary priorities?
- What can I drop from the plan?

Don't over-commit. Most weeks should have 1 big rock + 2 medium rocks.

## 3. Recurring themes (monthly)

Once a month, look back at 4 weeks of retros:

- What patterns appear in frustrations? (system, not luck)
- What patterns in wins? (do more of)
- Are my priorities trending in a consistent direction? Or scattered?

## 4. Energy audit

For the week:
- Energy gainers: [people, meetings, activities, hours of day]
- Energy drainers: [same categories]
- Net energy: surplus / breakeven / deficit?

Sustained energy deficit = burnout incoming. Take action before the crash.

## 5. Relationships audit

Who did I:
- Help? (relationship deposits)
- Lean on? (relationship withdrawals)
- Owe a reply / check-in?

Relationships compound; protect the most important ones.

## Rules

- **Do it weekly** — Friday afternoon or Sunday evening
- **15-30 min max** — not a chore, a tool
- **Write, don't think** — handwriting forces processing
- **Look back monthly** — pattern recognition kicks in
- **Share if helpful** — with manager (work), partner (life), coach

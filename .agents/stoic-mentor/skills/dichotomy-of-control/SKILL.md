# The Dichotomy of Control

Epictetus, Enchiridion 1:
> *"Some things are in our control and others not."*

When someone is anxious, frustrated, or stuck:

## Step 1: List the situation's elements

Whatever they bring you, break it into discrete items.

Example — "I'm stressed about the launch":
- The product quality
- Marketing budget
- Investor reactions
- Press coverage
- Customer adoption
- Competitor responses
- The weather on launch day

## Step 2: Sort each item

**In your control:**
- Your effort
- Your preparation
- Your response to events
- Your honesty
- Your reaction to outcomes

**Not in your control:**
- Other people's reactions
- Outcomes
- Past events
- Future certainty
- Weather, accidents, timing

## Step 3: Redirect attention

> *Focus 100% of effort on what's in your control. Let the rest go.*

This is not passivity. It's surgical attention.

## Step 4: One concrete action

Ask: "What's the ONE thing in your control you can do today?"

Just one. Not a plan. An action.

## Common traps

- "But surely my reputation is mine to control" — No. You control how you behave. Others decide your reputation.
- "I just need them to understand" — You can speak clearly. Their understanding is theirs.
- "If I work harder..." — Effort is yours. Outcome isn't.

When the trap appears, gently return to the dichotomy.

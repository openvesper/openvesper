# Customer Discovery

The trap: ask people what they want, they lie politely. Ask about their past behavior, they tell the truth.

## The Mom Test (Rob Fitzpatrick) — 3 rules

1. **Talk about their life, not your idea**
2. **Ask about specifics in the past, not generics or opinions about the future**
3. **Talk less, listen more**

## Good questions vs bad questions

### ❌ Bad questions (people lie)
- "Would you use a product that does X?" → confirmation bias
- "How much would you pay for X?" → people overestimate
- "Do you think this is a good idea?" → social desirability
- "Would you recommend X to a friend?" → hypothetical

### ✅ Good questions (people tell the truth)
- "Walk me through the last time you [did X]."
- "What's the hardest part about [job-to-be-done]?"
- "What have you tried to solve [problem]?"
- "How much time/money did you spend on [last attempt]?"
- "What would happen if you didn't solve this?"
- "Who else is affected by this?"

## Jobs-To-Be-Done framing

People hire products to do jobs. Understand:

1. **Functional job** — what they're trying to accomplish
2. **Emotional job** — how they want to feel (or avoid feeling)
3. **Social job** — how they want to be perceived

Example: Customer "hires" a $5 coffee not for caffeine (functional), but for the morning ritual (emotional) and the brand badge (social).

## Interview script template

```
INTRO (2 min)
"Thanks for the time. I'm researching [problem area]. There are no right or wrong
answers — I just want to understand your real experience. I'm not pitching anything."

WARM-UP (3 min)
"Tell me about your role / what you do."

PAST BEHAVIOR (15 min) — the meat
"Walk me through the last time you [encountered this problem]."
"What did you do?"
"What did you try?"
"What worked? What didn't?"
"How much time/money did this take?"
"Who else was involved?"

CURRENT STATE (10 min)
"Today, when this problem comes up, what's your process?"
"What tools/people do you use?"
"What part frustrates you?"
"What would make this easier?" (only after they've described problems)

DEEP DIVE on biggest pain (10 min)
"Tell me more about [specific pain they mentioned]."
"How often does that happen?"
"What's the cost when it does?"
"Have you ever tried to solve it? What happened?"

ASKS (2 min)
"Anyone else I should talk to about this?"
"Can I follow up if I have more questions?"
```

## Signal vs noise

### 🚨 Strong signals (pursue)
- They've already tried multiple solutions
- They've spent money on workarounds
- They have a hack/spreadsheet/script to deal with it
- Their voice changes when discussing the pain
- They volunteer specific numbers (time wasted, cost)

### ⚠️ Weak signals (probe more)
- "Yeah, that would be cool"
- "I'd definitely use that"
- "Sounds interesting"
- Polite enthusiasm without specifics

### ❌ Anti-signals (deprioritize)
- They can't recall a specific recent instance
- They've never paid for any solution
- The problem only matters "sometimes"
- They're suggesting features (designing the product for you)

## Rules

- **Don't sell** — once you pitch, the conversation contaminates
- **Be quiet** — 70% listening, 30% asking
- **Follow specifics** — when they mention something interesting, dig in
- **Note exact phrases** — "lock that down" or "manual hell" become your copy
- **5-10 interviews** before pattern recognition kicks in
- **Re-interview after building** — same people, did you solve the right thing?

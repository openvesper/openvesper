# MEDDPICC Deal Review

For each letter, ask:

## M — Metrics
- What quantifiable result does the customer want? (revenue +X%, cost -Y%, time -Z hours)
- Have they shared a NUMBER, or are they hand-waving?
- **If no metric: deal isn't real yet.** Ask: "How will you measure success in 12 months?"

## E — Economic buyer
- WHO can write the check / sign the contract?
- Have you spoken to them DIRECTLY?
- **If only champions: ask champion to introduce.** "Who else needs to weigh in for this to move forward?"

## D — Decision criteria
- What features/qualities matter to THEM (not what you sell)?
- Ranked by importance?
- **Don't assume your value prop matches their criteria.** Ask: "If you evaluated 3 vendors, what would tip you to one?"

## D — Decision process
- Who's involved? (procurement, security, legal, IT, executive)
- Timeline? Sequence?
- **If unclear: deal will surprise you.** Ask: "Walk me through what happens after you decide to move forward."

## P — Paper process
- Procurement, security review, legal — how long?
- Vendor approval, MSAs, SOC2 needs
- **For enterprise: paper takes 30-90 days easy.** Ask: "When does procurement get involved?"

## I — Identify pain
- WHAT specific pain are they feeling? (lost revenue, manual work, errors, risk)
- How long has it been going on?
- What's the cost of inaction?
- **If they can't articulate the pain: not urgent.** Ask: "What happens if you do nothing for 6 more months?"

## C — Champion
- Who internally is FIGHTING for this?
- Are they powerful? (Can they influence the EB?)
- Are they sold? Tested?
- **Test the champion:** "If your boss said no tomorrow, what would you do?"

## C — Competition
- What's the alternative? (Status quo, internal build, competing vendor)
- Where are they in evaluation with competitors?
- **Status quo is the #1 competitor.** Ask: "What have you tried before?"

❓ GAPS: [letters with missing info, ranked]
